<p><p>The spectroscopy instruments market includes various techniques for analyzing material properties. Raman spectroscopy utilizes inelastic scattering of light to provide molecular information. Fluorescence spectroscopy measures emitted light from a substance after excitation, revealing its composition. Near-infrared spectroscopy assesses molecular vibrations in the near-infrared range, useful for organic compounds. Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) Spectroscopy captures spectral data by measuring absorbance at various wavelengths, enabling material characterization. Other techniques encompass UV-Vis spectroscopy, mass spectrometry, and laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy, broadening analytical capabilities across industries.</p></p>
