<p><p>Structured packing is a type of packing used in separation processes such as distillation, absorption, and extraction. <p><p>Structured packing, used in chemical separation processes, is categorized into three market types: metal, plastic, and ceramics. Metal structured packing, often made from stainless steel or alloys, offers high durability and thermal stability, suitable for harsh conditions. Plastic structured packing, typically made from materials like polypropylene, is lightweight and resistant to corrosion, making it ideal for less demanding environments. Ceramic structured packing, known for its high resistance to aggressive chemicals and heat, is commonly used in specialized applications requiring excellent performance and longevity.</p></p>
