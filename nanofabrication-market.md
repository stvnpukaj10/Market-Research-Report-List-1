<p><ul><li>Traditional Processing</li><li>Non-traditional Processing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nanofabrication market is segmented into traditional and non-traditional processing techniques. Traditional processing involves established methods like photolithography and etching, which use light or chemicals to pattern nanoscale structures on substrates. In contrast, non-traditional processing encompasses newer techniques such as self-assembly, nanoimprint lithography, and chemical vapor deposition, which offer novel approaches to creating nanomaterials and structures. Both types cater to various applications, including electronics, biomedicine, and materials science, addressing the growing demand for advanced nanotechnology solutions.</p></p>
